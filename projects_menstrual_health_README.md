# Menstrual Health Awareness Survey Analysis

## 📋 Project Overview

This project analyzes survey data on menstrual health awareness, myths, and product knowledge across 60+ respondents. The goal was to identify awareness gaps, understand which menstrual myths are most prevalent, and assess attitudes toward open discussion and education.

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Menstrual health is often a taboo topic in many societies. This survey aimed to understand:
- How aware are people of menstrual health facts vs. myths?
- What are the most common misconceptions?
- What's the appetite for education and open conversation?
- Are there demographic differences in awareness levels?

## 📊 Key Findings

✅ **80%+ of respondents** strongly agree that open discussions about menstruation should be encouraged

✅ **Common myths identified:**
- Avoiding religious places during menstruation
- Avoiding certain foods
- Avoiding physical activities/sports
- Sleeping separately

✅ **Product awareness is high** — tampons, menstrual cups, and period underwear are known by 70%+ of respondents

✅ **Educational gap:** School is the primary source of menstrual education (40%+), but many still rely on family or internet/social media

## 📈 Insights for Action

1. **School curricula should prioritize menstrual education** — making it mandatory and comprehensive
2. **Campaign messaging:** Focus on debunking the top 3-4 myths with targeted awareness campaigns
3. **Product accessibility:** Ensure all menstrual product types are available and affordable across regions
4. **Normalize conversations:** Leverage the high agreement on open discussion to build community-led initiatives
